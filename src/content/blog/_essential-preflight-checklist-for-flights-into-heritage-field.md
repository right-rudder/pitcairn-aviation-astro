Preflight planning is critical for a safe and smooth arrival at Heritage Field Airport (KPTW). Pitcairn Aviation recommends a thorough checklist for all pilots, ensuring both aircraft readiness and compliance with regulations.

## 1. Aircraft Inspection

- Check fuel levels and fuel quality
- Inspect oil, hydraulic fluids, and battery condition
- Verify control surfaces, tires, and landing gear
- Confirm that avionics and navigation equipment are functional

## 2. Flight Planning

- Review current METARs and TAFs for KPTW and en route airports
- Calculate weight and balance for the flight
- Check NOTAMs for any runway closures or airspace restrictions
- File a flight plan if required for IFR or cross-country operations

## 3. Safety Equipment and Documents

- Ensure all required documents are on board (airworthiness certificate, registration, pilot license, medical certificate)
- Verify emergency equipment (fire extinguisher, first aid kit, ELT) is accessible and operational
- Confirm survival equipment if flying over water or remote areas

## 4. Pilot Preparation

- Review the expected approach and departure procedures at KPTW
- Plan for alternative airports in case of weather changes
- Check for any temporary airspace restrictions
- Ensure personal readiness, including rest, hydration, and briefing passengers

## 5. Coordination with Pitcairn Aviation

- Schedule fueling, hangar, or tie-down services if needed
- Confirm ground transportation arrangements
- Ask Pitcairn Aviation staff for any local updates or airport-specific tips

## Final Thoughts

Using a structured preflight checklist reduces risk, improves efficiency, and enhances your overall flight experience into KPTW. Pitcairn Aviation supports pilots at every step, ensuring **safe, convenient, and enjoyable arrivals** at Heritage Field Airport.
